# Chapter 10: Brown and the Backlash (1953–1963)

## Overview

**Time Span:** 1953–1963 (10 years)

**One-Paragraph Description:**
_Brown v. Board of Education_ (1954) declares school segregation unconstitutional, but _Brown II_ (1955) delays enforcement with "all deliberate speed," resulting in massive resistance. Southern states defy federal courts. In Little Rock (1957), Eisenhower deploys the 101st Airborne—the only time in U.S. history federal troops enforce a Supreme Court order against state resistance. _Cooper v. Aaron_ (1958) is an unprecedented reassertion of supremacy. A decade later, fewer than 2% of Black Southern students attend desegregated schools. Klarman's backlash thesis: _Brown_ mobilized white opposition, which in turn produced the legislative achievements (1964/1965 Civil Rights acts) that mattered more than the Court's ruling.

## Driving Question

Can the Court desegregate the South by judicial decree?

## Lenses Deployed

- **Primary Lens (Democracy vs. Supremacy):** Court asserts but cannot enforce; requires presidential action.
- **Secondary Lens 1 (Legitimacy):** Massive resistance in South vs. support in North; record levels of Black hope and frustration.
- **Secondary Lens 2 (Race):** _Brown_ as catalyst for backlash more than direct change.
- **Supporting Lens (Rights Cycles):** Expansion phase begins.

## Backlash Cycle: Brown and Massive Resistance

| Beat                 | Event                                                                 | Outcome                                                             |
| :------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Decision**         | Brown I (1954) declares segregation unconstitutional.                 | Court asserts supremacy over Southern states.                       |
| **Reaction**         | Massive Resistance; school closures; Faubus blocks integration.       | 101st Airborne deploys; Cooper v. Aaron (1958) reasserts supremacy. |
| **Counter-Reaction** | 10 years later, only 2% desegregation; but backlash produces VRA/CRA. | Movement victories matter more than judicial decree.                |
