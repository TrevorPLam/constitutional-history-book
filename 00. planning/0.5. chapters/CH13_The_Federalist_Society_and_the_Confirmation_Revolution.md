# Chapter 13: The Federalist Society and the Confirmation Revolution (1980–1992)

## Overview

**Time Span:** 1980–1992 (12 years)

**One-Paragraph Description:**
The Federalist Society is founded in 1982, creating an ideological network that will eventually dominate the judiciary. Reagan makes judicial selection a strategic priority. The Bork hearings (1987) are the hinge: his defeat teaches the conservative movement that control of the Court is worth fighting for at any cost and that nominees must be more careful. Rehnquist becomes Chief Justice. The confirmation process is forever transformed—no longer about qualifications but about constitutional vision. Legitimacy becomes increasingly partisan.

## Driving Question

Can a self-conscious ideological movement capture the judiciary?
