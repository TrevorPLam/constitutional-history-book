# Chapter 19: Supremacy Without Accountability (2023–Present)

## Overview

**Time Span:** 2023–Present (3 years)

**One-Paragraph Description:**
The Court exercises supremacy more broadly than ever while its legitimacy reservoir is empty. _Loper Bright_ (2024) ends 40 years of _Chevron_ deference, transferring regulatory power from the executive to the courts. _Trump v. United States_ (2024) grants broad presidential immunity for "official acts," testing the separation of powers. ProPublica reveals undisclosed gifts to Thomas and Alito, sparking an ethics crisis that the Court's unenforced 2023 Code of Conduct fails to resolve. The shadow docket governs major areas (immigration, environment) through unsigned orders. The machinery of judicial power—dark money, amicus coordination, and clerk pipelines—is now fully visible to a skeptical public.

## Driving Question

Can the Court maintain supremacy when its internal machinery becomes publicly visible?

## Lenses Deployed

- **Primary Lens (Democracy vs. Supremacy):** Court asserts supremacy without restraint; legitimacy collapses.
- **Secondary Lens 1 (Legitimacy):** Ethics crisis (Thomas/Alito); record lows in trust.
- **Supporting Lens (Economic Power):** _Loper Bright_ and regulatory rollback.
- **Supporting Lens (Shadow Docket):** Governance by emergency injunction.
- **Supporting Lens (Regime Politics):** Court aligned with conservative movement despite public divergence.
