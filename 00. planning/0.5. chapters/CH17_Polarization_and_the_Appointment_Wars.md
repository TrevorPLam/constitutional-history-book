# Chapter 17: Polarization and the Appointment Wars (2013–2020)

## Overview

**Time Span:** 2013–2020 (7 years)

**One-Paragraph Description:**
The 2016 blocking of Merrick Garland represents a collapse of Senate norms. Trump wins, and the appointments of Gorsuch, Kavanaugh, and Barrett entrench a 6-3 conservative supermajority. Kavanaugh's confirmation becomes a cultural referendum, and Barrett is confirmed just weeks before the 2020 election. Meanwhile, the shadow docket expands as the Trump administration repeatedly seeks (and the Court grants) emergency relief without full briefing or oral argument. Legitimacy plummets to record lows as the machinery of judicial power—appointment timing, dark money, and emergency orders—becomes visible to the public.

## Driving Question

What happens when the confirmation process becomes total war?

## Lenses Deployed

- **Primary Lens (Democracy vs. Supremacy):** Hardball politics determines Court composition.
- **Secondary Lens 1 (Legitimacy):** Record low public trust; partisan chasm.
- **Supporting Lens (Confirmation Process):** Total collapse of norms (Garland/Barrett).
- **Supporting Lens (Shadow Docket):** Governance by emergency injunction.
- **Supporting Lens (Regime Politics):** 6-3 supermajority as partisan entrenchment.
