# Epilogue: Who Decides?

## Overview

**One-Paragraph Description:**
The epilogue synthesizes the full arc and poses the fundamental question directly: judicial supremacy is a construction, not a constitutional inevitability—so should it be preserved, reformed, or replaced? Survey the proposed reforms: term limits, court expansion, jurisdiction stripping, enforceable ethics codes, and democratizing constitutional interpretation. Place them in comparative context: Canada's notwithstanding clause, the UK's weak-form review, and Germany's supermajority appointments. The book concludes that the balance between the Court's supremacy claims and public acceptance is broken, and what comes next is a political choice, not a legal inevitability.

## Driving Question

After 235 years, who should have the last word on constitutional meaning?

## Lenses Deployed

- **Synthesis of All Lenses:** How the democracy-supremacy tension, legitimacy, and race all converge in the modern crisis.
- **Comparative Constitutional Context:** Strong-form vs. weak-form review.
- **Popular Constitutionalism:** The people as a live option for constitutional interpretation.

## Gap Integration

- **Gap 9: Comparative Constitutionalism (Strong-Form vs. Weak-Form Review).**
  - Integrate: Reframing the American model as an outlier; using Canada (notwithstanding) and the UK/NZ models as alternatives that preserve review but maintain accountability.
- **Gap 10: Jurisdiction Stripping as Democratic Check.**
  - Integrate: Re-evaluating Article III, Section 2 as a textual (if politically difficult) tool for democratic control.
- **Gap 11: State Constitutional Counter-Mobilization (Post-Dobbs).**
  - Integrate: State constitutionalism as a form of "local" weak-form review that is already functioning within the US system.
- **Gap 8: Gender and the Unfinished Constitution.**
  - Integrate: The ERA as a reform that would provide the missing textual anchor for equality.
