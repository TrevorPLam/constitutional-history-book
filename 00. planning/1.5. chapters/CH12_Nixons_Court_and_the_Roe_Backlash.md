# Chapter 12: Nixon's Court and the Roe Backlash (1969–1980)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the beginning of the conservative legal project through Nixon's Court appointments and the cultural war triggered by Roe v. Wade. The Burger Court represents the first systematic conservative effort to capture judicial supremacy and use it to roll back the Warren Revolution, establishing the template for modern Court politics.

### Core Argument
Nixon's Court demonstrates judicial supremacy as a tool of conservative transformation. By appointing justices committed to overturning Warren-era precedents, Nixon shows how judicial power can be captured and used to reverse democratic progress, creating the polarization that defines modern Court politics.
