# Chapter 16: Roberts's Quiet Revolution (2005–2013)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the Roberts Court's sophisticated transformation of American constitutional law through strategic minimalism and procedural innovation. The Roberts era represents a new form of judicial supremacy — less visible than previous revolutions but more fundamental in its restructuring of American governance.

### Core Argument
Roberts' quiet revolution demonstrates judicial supremacy's evolution from dramatic transformation to subtle restructuring. By using narrow decisions and procedural control rather than broad pronouncements, the Roberts Court achieves revolutionary change while maintaining appearance of judicial restraint, creating a more durable form of judicial authority.

### Chapter Structure

#### Part I: The Roberts Strategy
- **Chief Justice's vision**: Transforming law through minimalism
- **Procedural focus**: Shadow docket and agenda-setting as primary tools
- **Coalition building**: Five-justice conservative majority with strategic swing votes
- **The innovation**: Quiet revolution through technical precision

#### Part II: The Minimalist Revolution
- **Citizens United (2010)**: Corporate political speech transformation
- **Shelby County v. Holder (2013)**: Voting rights rollback through technical means
- **Burwell v. Hobby Lobby (2014)**: Religious rights expansion
- **The pattern**: Narrow decisions with broad systemic consequences

#### Part III: The Shadow Docket Expansion
- **Emergency orders**: Major policy changes without full briefing
- **Stay authority**: Controlling lower court decisions nationally
- **Procedural innovation**: Using shadow docket for policy-making
- **The power**: Governing through silence and procedure

#### Part IV: The Conservative Achievement
- **Healthcare reform**: NFIB v. Sebelius (2012) limits Medicaid expansion
- **Public sector**: Janus v. AFSCME (2018) weakens unions
- **Regulatory rollback**: West Virginia v. EPA (2022) limits administrative power
- **The result**: Systematic conservative transformation of governance

#### Part V: The Quiet Revolution's Significance
- **Strategic brilliance**: Achieving major change with minimal political cost
- **Public perception**: Court appears moderate while being radically transformative
- **Institutional impact**: New model of judicial supremacy
- **The foundation**: Template for future conservative judicial governance
