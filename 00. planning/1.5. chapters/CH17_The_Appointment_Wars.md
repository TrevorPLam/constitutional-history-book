# Chapter 17: The Appointment Wars (2013–2020)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the escalation of confirmation battles to total political warfare and the Roberts Court's expansion of shadow docket power. The appointment wars represent the final stage in the transformation of judicial supremacy from constitutional interpretation to raw political power.

### Core Argument
The appointment wars demonstrate judicial supremacy's ultimate expression as political power. When confirmations become pure partisan warfare and the Court simultaneously expands its procedural authority, judicial supremacy transforms from constitutional interpretation to direct political governance.

### Chapter Structure

#### Part I: The Escalation to Warfare
- **Garland blockade**: Republican refusal to consider Obama nominee
- **Nuclear option**: Democratic elimination of filibuster for lower court appointments
- **The transformation**: Confirmation from professional evaluation to partisan combat
- **The new normal**: Total warfare over judicial appointments

#### Part II: The Trump Court Revolution
- **Gorsuch confirmation**: Nuclear option and partisan seating
- **Kavanaugh confirmation**: Sexual assault allegations and partisan confirmation
- **The achievement**: Solid 6-3 conservative majority through warfare
- **The cost**: Complete destruction of confirmation norms

#### Part III: The Shadow Docket Explosion
- **COVID-19 orders**: Religious liberty victories without argument
- **Election cases**: Partisan intervention in election administration
- **Immigration restrictions**: Policy implementation through procedural orders
- **The power**: Governing through emergency authority

#### Part IV: The Court as Political Actor
- **Partisan decisions**: Conservative victories along ideological lines
- **Procedural innovation**: Using shadow docket for policy implementation
- **Public legitimacy**: Court seen as partisan rather than judicial
- **The reality**: Supreme Court as political branch

#### Part V: The New Constitutional Order
- **Judicial supremacy achieved**: Court as final policy authority
- **Political question doctrine**: Selective deference to elected branches
- **Institutional transformation**: Court as political governing body
- **The foundation**: New equilibrium of judicial-political power
