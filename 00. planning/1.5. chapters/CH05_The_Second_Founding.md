# Chapter 5: The Second Founding (1865–1877)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines how Reconstruction attempted to remake American constitutional order through judicial enforcement of revolutionary amendments. The Reconstruction Court represents the first systematic effort to use judicial supremacy to protect democracy rather than limit it, creating a model of constitutional transformation that would be systematically dismantled.

### Core Argument
The Reconstruction Court demonstrates judicial supremacy's democratic potential. By enforcing the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments, the Court showed how judicial power could protect vulnerable minorities and enforce democratic commitments. The subsequent betrayal of this vision reveals the contingency of judicial protection for democracy.

### Chapter Structure

#### Part I: The Constitutional Revolution
- **War's constitutional legacy**: 13th, 14th, 15th Amendments
- **Revolutionary purpose**: Transforming American democracy and citizenship
- **Judicial role**: Court as enforcer of new constitutional order
- **The opportunity**: Using judicial supremacy for democratic ends

#### Part II: The Early Reconstruction Court
- **Initial enthusiasm**: Judicial enforcement of Reconstruction amendments
- **Key cases**: Early civil rights decisions and voting rights protection
- **Legal innovation**: Developing constitutional doctrines for new amendments
- **Democratic promise**: Judicial power as shield for newly freed citizens

#### Part III: The Antisubordination Breakthrough
- **The Slaughterhouse Cases (1873)**: Beginning of judicial retreat
- **Narrowing 14th Amendment**: Privileges or immunities limited
- **The turning point**: Court begins abandoning Reconstruction promise
- **Legal reasoning**: Formalistic interpretation undermining democratic purpose

#### Part IV: The Betrayal Begins
- **United States v. Cruikshank (1876)**: Undermining federal civil rights protection
- **United States v. Reese (1876)**: Limiting 15th Amendment enforcement
- **The pattern**: Court systematically dismantles Reconstruction protections
- **The consequences**: Return to white supremacist control in the South

#### Part V: The End of Reconstruction
- **Political context**: Northern retreat, Southern resistance, Supreme Court acquiescence
- **Compromise of 1877**: End of federal Reconstruction
- **Judicial role**: Court provides legal cover for retreat
- **The legacy**: Unfulfilled constitutional promise and democratic setback

### Key Sources and Evidence
- **Reconstruction amendments**: Text and legislative history
- **Court opinions**: Full text of major Reconstruction decisions
- **Congressional records**: Reconstruction debates and abandonment
- **Contemporary newspapers**: Northern and Southern reactions to Court decisions

### Integration with Book's Themes
- **Democracy vs. Supremacy**: Court initially uses supremacy to protect democracy
- **Legitimacy measurement**: Initial support followed by growing disillusionment
- **Backlash cycle**: Reconstruction promise → Judicial retreat → Jim Crow equilibrium
- **Race lens**: Antisubordination vs. anticlassification through-line established

### Chapter Significance
This chapter shows judicial supremacy's democratic potential and its vulnerability. The Reconstruction Court demonstrates that judicial power can protect democracy, but also that this protection depends on judicial will rather than institutional structure.

### Backlash Preview (Part II Opening)
The Reconstruction era reveals a complex backlash cycle: constitutional revolution (13th-15th Amendments) → judicial protection (early Reconstruction Court) → counter-revolution (Slaughterhouse to Cruikshank) → new equilibrium (Jim Crow). This pattern shows how judicial supremacy can both advance and undermine democratic progress.

### Connection to Subsequent Chapters
- Establishes the democratic promise that Chapter 6's Betrayal systematically destroys
- Creates the constitutional framework that Chapter 7's Gilded Court ignores
- Sets the civil rights precedent that Chapter 10's Brown Court revives
