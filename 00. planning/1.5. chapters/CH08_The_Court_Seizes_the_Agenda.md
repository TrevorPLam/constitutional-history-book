# Chapter 8: The Court Seizes the Agenda (1916–1937)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the Supreme Court's transformation from passive reviewer to active agenda-setter during the Progressive Era and New Deal. The Court moves beyond interpreting law to shaping national policy, claiming authority to determine what democratic majorities can accomplish. This chapter includes the crucial comparative mirror showing how the New Deal crisis represents America's closest approach to weak-form review.

### Core Argument
The Court's agenda-setting power represents a new form of judicial supremacy — not just interpreting law but determining what law is possible. By striking down democratic innovations, the Court establishes itself as the final arbiter of national policy, creating a constitutional crisis that can only be resolved through political confrontation.
