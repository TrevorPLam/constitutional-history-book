# Chapter 14: The Rehnquist Court's Federalism Revolution (1986–2000)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the Rehnquist Court's systematic revival of federalism and states' rights as conservative constitutional weapons. The Rehnquist era represents the sophisticated deployment of judicial supremacy to rollback federal power and empower conservative governance, establishing the foundation for post-Dobbs state constitutionalism.

### Core Argument
The Rehnquist federalism revolution demonstrates judicial supremacy's capacity to restructure constitutional architecture. By resurrecting states' rights and limiting federal power, the Court shows how judicial authority can be used to remake federalism as a tool of conservative governance rather than neutral federalism.
