# Chapter 13: The Federalist Society and the Confirmation Revolution (1980–1992)

### Chapter Overview
This chapter examines the institutionalization of conservative judicial power through the Federalist Society and the transformation of confirmation battles into ideological warfare. The Reagan Court represents the maturation of Nixon's conservative project into a sophisticated infrastructure for capturing and maintaining judicial supremacy.

### Core Argument
The Federalist Society revolution demonstrates how judicial supremacy can be systematically captured and maintained through organized ideological infrastructure. By building a complete pipeline from law school to the Supreme Court, conservatives create a self-reinforcing system that makes judicial authority a permanent conservative advantage.

### Chapter Significance
This chapter shows the transformation of Court politics from episodic battles to systematic warfare. The Federalist Society demonstrates how organized ideological infrastructure can make judicial supremacy a permanent partisan advantage rather than a neutral constitutional arbiter.

### Backlash Preview (Part IV Opening)
The Reagan era reveals the infrastructure backlash cycle: systematic organization (Federalist Society) → confirmation warfare → conservative Court → institutionalized advantage. This pattern shows how judicial supremacy can be permanently captured through comprehensive ideological infrastructure.
