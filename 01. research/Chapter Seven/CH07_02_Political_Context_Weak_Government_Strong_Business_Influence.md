# CH07: Political Context: Weak Government, Strong Business Influence

## Overview
The Gilded Age political context featured weak government institutions and strong business influence, creating conditions where judicial protection of corporate interests could flourish with minimal democratic resistance.

## Primary Sources
### Government Weakness
- **Limited Federal Power**: Federal government lacked capacity to regulate corporate behavior.
- **Political Corruption**: Business interests dominated political processes through corruption.
- **Regulatory Capture**: Government agencies controlled by business interests.

### Business Influence
- **Corporate Lobbying**: Systematic business influence over legislative processes.
- **Campaign Finance**: Business money dominated political campaigns.
- **Revolving Door**: Movement between corporate and government positions.
