# CH07: Social Conflict: Labor Struggles, Populist Movements, Progressive Reform

## Overview
The Gilded Age experienced intense social conflict through labor struggles, populist movements, and progressive reform efforts that challenged corporate power and demanded democratic regulation of the economy.

## Primary Sources
### Labor Struggles
- **Union Organization**: Workers organized to challenge corporate power.
- **Strikes and Protests**: Massive labor actions against working conditions.
- **Industrial Conflict**: Violent clashes between workers and corporate interests.

### Populist Movements
- **Agrarian Protest**: Farmers organized against corporate and financial interests.
- **Political Mobilization**: Third parties challenged establishment control.
- **Economic Reform Demands**: Calls for regulation of banks and railroads.

### Progressive Reform
- **Urban Reform**: City-based movements against corruption and corporate control.
- **Social Justice**: Reformers addressed inequality and working conditions.
- **Government Regulation**: Demands for democratic control of economy.
