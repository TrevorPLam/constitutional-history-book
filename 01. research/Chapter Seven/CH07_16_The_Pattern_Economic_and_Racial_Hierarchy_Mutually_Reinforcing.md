# CH07: The Pattern: Economic and Racial Hierarchy Mutually Reinforcing

## Overview
The Supreme Court's Gilded Age decisions created a pattern where economic and racial hierarchy were mutually reinforcing, with the Court simultaneously protecting corporate economic power while maintaining racial subordination.

## Primary Sources
### Dual Protection System
- **Corporate Rights Protected**: Court used 14th Amendment to shield business interests.
- **Racial Subordination Maintained**: Court upheld segregation and disenfranchisement.
- **Hierarchical Order**: Both economic and racial hierarchies constitutionally protected.

### Legal Framework
- **Constitutional Dualism**: Different constitutional doctrines for economic vs. racial cases.
- **Hierarchy Protection**: Court served as guardian of multiple forms of subordination.
- **Elite Power Shield**: Both corporate and white supremacist interests constitutionally protected.
