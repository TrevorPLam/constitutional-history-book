# CH07: Lochner v. New York (1905): Freedom of Contract as Constitutional Right

## Overview
Lochner v. New York (1905) established freedom of contract as a constitutional right, creating the Lochner era where the Supreme Court protected economic liberty through substantive due process and systematically struck down labor regulations.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Lochner v. New York**: 198 U.S. 45 (1905) - freedom of contract established as constitutional right.
- **Baking Hours Law Struck Down**: New York law limiting bakers' working hours declared unconstitutional.
- **Substantive Due Process**: Court protected economic liberty under due process clause.

### Legal Framework
- **Freedom of Contract Doctrine**: Right to contract protected as fundamental liberty.
- **Economic Due Process**: Due process clause applied to protect economic interests.
- **Labor Regulation Invalidated**: Government regulation of working conditions struck down.
