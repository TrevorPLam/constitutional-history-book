# CH07: The Doctrine: Court Imposes Economic Theory on Democratic Decisions

## Overview
The Supreme Court's Lochner era doctrine imposed economic theory on democratic decisions, using judicial authority to override legislative judgments about economic regulation and protect business interests from democratic control.

## Primary Sources
### Economic Theory Imposition
- **Judicial Economic Doctrine**: Court imposed laissez-faire economic theory on legislation.
- **Democratic Override**: Judicial decisions trumped democratic economic regulation.
- **Business Protection**: Court used constitutional interpretation to protect business interests.

### Legal Framework
- **Economic Liberty Doctrine**: Court defined economic liberty as constitutional right.
- **Legislative Judgment Replaced**: Court substituted its economic judgment for legislative decisions.
- **Constitutional Economics**: Court created constitutional framework for economic regulation.
