# CH07: Williams v. Mississippi (1898): Disenfranchisement Upheld

## Overview
Williams v. Mississippi (1898) upheld Mississippi's disenfranchisement constitution, providing constitutional legitimacy for Southern laws that effectively eliminated Black voting rights through procedural barriers.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Williams v. Mississippi**: 170 U.S. 213 (1898) - disenfranchisement constitution upheld.
- **Procedural Barriers Validated**: Poll taxes, literacy tests, and residency requirements approved.
- **15th Amendment Limited**: Court refused to protect Black voting rights from state restrictions.

### Legal Framework
- **Disenfranchisement Constitution**: Mississippi's 1890 constitution constitutionally protected.
- **Procedural Discrimination**: Court upheld facially neutral but racially discriminatory voting restrictions.
- **State Voting Power**: States given broad authority to regulate voting qualifications.
