# CH07: Judicial Role: Court as Guardian of Economic Order

## Overview
The Supreme Court assumed the role of guardian of economic order during the Gilded Age, using judicial authority to protect corporate interests and economic hierarchy from democratic regulation and social reform movements.

## Primary Sources
### Judicial Protection
- **Corporate Shield**: Court used constitutional doctrines to protect business interests.
- **Economic Order Maintenance**: Judicial decisions preserved existing economic hierarchies.
- **Democratic Regulation Blocked**: Court systematically struck down regulatory efforts.

### Constitutional Authority
- **14th Amendment Application**: Equal protection used to shield corporate interests.
- **Due Process Expansion**: Substantive due process protected economic liberty.
- **Judicial Supremacy**: Court asserted authority over economic regulation.
