# CH07: Labor Regulation Struck Down: Minimum Wage, Working Hours, Union Rights

## Overview
The Supreme Court systematically struck down labor regulations including minimum wage laws, working hour limits, and union rights, using substantive due process to protect business interests from democratic regulation.

## Primary Sources
### Labor Regulation Invalidated
- **Minimum Wage Laws**: Court struck down minimum wage regulations as unconstitutional.
- **Working Hour Limits**: Laws limiting working hours invalidated under freedom of contract.
- **Union Rights Protected**: Court protected employers' rights to resist unionization.

### Legal Framework
- **Freedom of Contract Applied**: Labor regulations struck down as interfering with contract freedom.
- **Economic Liberty Protected**: Business interests shielded from labor regulation.
- **Judicial Economic Theory**: Court imposed economic theory on democratic decisions.
