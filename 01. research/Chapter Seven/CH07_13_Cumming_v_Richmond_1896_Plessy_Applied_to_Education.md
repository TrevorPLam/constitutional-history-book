# CH07: Cumming v. Richmond (1896): Plessy Applied to Education

## Overview
Cumming v. Richmond County Board of Education (1896) extended the "separate but equal" doctrine to education, applying Plessy v. Ferguson to schools and constitutionalizing educational segregation.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Cumming v. Richmond**: 175 U.S. 528 (1896) - Plessy doctrine applied to education.
- **School Segregation Upheld**: Court upheld separate schools for Black and white students.
- **Educational Equality Facade**: "Separate but equal" applied to educational facilities.

### Legal Framework
- **Plessy Extension**: "Separate but equal" doctrine extended to education.
- **Educational Segregation**: Constitutional protection for school segregation.
- **State Education Power**: States given authority to maintain segregated schools.
