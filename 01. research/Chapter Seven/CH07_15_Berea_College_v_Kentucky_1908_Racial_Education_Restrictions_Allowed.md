# CH07: Berea College v. Kentucky (1908): Racial Education Restrictions Allowed

## Overview
Berea College v. Kentucky (1908) upheld state laws prohibiting integrated education, allowing states to ban private educational institutions from teaching Black and white students together.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Berea College v. Kentucky**: 211 U.S. 45 (1908) - racial education restrictions allowed.
- **Private Education Segregation**: States given authority to regulate private educational integration.
- **Academic Freedom Limited**: College's right to integrated education constitutionally restricted.

### Legal Framework
- **State Education Power**: States' authority to regulate education expanded to private institutions.
- **Racial Separation Mandated**: Court upheld laws requiring racial separation in education.
- **Academic Freedom Subordinate**: Educational freedom subordinated to segregationist state policy.
