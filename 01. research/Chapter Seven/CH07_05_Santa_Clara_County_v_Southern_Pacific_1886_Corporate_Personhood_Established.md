# CH07: Santa Clara County v. Southern Pacific (1886): Corporate Personhood Established

## Overview
Santa Clara County v. Southern Pacific (1886) marked the pivotal moment when corporate personhood was established, granting corporations constitutional protections under the 14th Amendment and fundamentally transforming American constitutional law.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Santa Clara County v. Southern Pacific**: 118 U.S. 394 (1886) - corporate personhood recognized.
- **Court Reporter Note**: Reporter's headnote declared corporations are "persons" under 14th Amendment.
- **Constitutional Recognition**: Corporations received equal protection clause benefits.

### Legal Framework
- **Corporate Personhood Doctrine**: Corporations recognized as constitutional "persons."
- **14th Amendment Extension**: Equal protection applied to corporate entities.
- **Legal Revolution**: Corporations gained same constitutional rights as human citizens.
