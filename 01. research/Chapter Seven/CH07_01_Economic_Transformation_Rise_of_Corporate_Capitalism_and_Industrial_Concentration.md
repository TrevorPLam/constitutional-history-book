# CH07: Economic Transformation: Rise of Corporate Capitalism and Industrial Concentration

## Overview
The Gilded Age witnessed unprecedented economic transformation with the rise of corporate capitalism and industrial concentration, creating new economic hierarchies that required constitutional protection from democratic regulation.

## Primary Sources
### Corporate Capitalism Development
- **Industrial Concentration**: Massive corporations dominated key sectors of the economy.
- **Trust Formation**: Large business combinations controlled entire industries.
- **Economic Power Consolidation**: Wealth and economic authority concentrated in few hands.

### Economic Context
- **Railroad Expansion**: Transportation networks enabled national markets.
- **Technological Innovation**: New production methods increased scale and efficiency.
- **Financial Markets**: Capital markets funded massive industrial expansion.
