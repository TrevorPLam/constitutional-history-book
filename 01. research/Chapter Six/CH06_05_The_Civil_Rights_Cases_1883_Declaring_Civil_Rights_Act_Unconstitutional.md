# CH06: The Civil Rights Cases (1883): Declaring Civil Rights Act Unconstitutional

## Overview
The Civil Rights Cases (1883) represented a decisive blow to Reconstruction by declaring the Civil Rights Act of 1875 unconstitutional, establishing the "state action" doctrine that would cripple federal civil rights protection for nearly a century.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **The Civil Rights Cases**: 109 U.S. 3 (1883) - declared Civil Rights Act unconstitutional.
- **State Action Doctrine**: Court held 14th Amendment only applied to state action, not private discrimination.
- **Congressional Power Limited**: Decision restricted congressional authority to protect civil rights.

### Legal Reasoning
- **Narrow Construction**: Court narrowly interpreted 14th Amendment's scope.
- **Private Discrimination Protected**: Decision effectively immunized private racial discrimination.
- **Federal Authority Restricted**: Congress lost power to regulate private racial discrimination.
