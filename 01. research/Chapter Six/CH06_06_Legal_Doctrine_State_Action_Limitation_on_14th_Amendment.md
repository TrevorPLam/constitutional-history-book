# CH06: Legal Doctrine: "State Action" Limitation on 14th Amendment

## Overview
The "state action" doctrine established in the Civil Rights Cases severely limited the 14th Amendment's reach, holding that constitutional protections only applied to government action, not private discrimination.

## Primary Sources
### State Action Doctrine
- **Constitutional Limitation**: 14th Amendment only restricted state, not private, action.
- **Private Discrimination Protected**: Decision immunized private racial discrimination from constitutional challenge.
- **Federal Power Restricted**: Congress lost authority to regulate private racial discrimination.

### Legal Framework
- **Government Action Requirement**: Constitutional protections only triggered by state action.
- **Private Sphere Immunity**: Private discrimination placed beyond constitutional reach.
- **Federalism Emphasis**: Decision prioritized state autonomy over federal civil rights protection.
