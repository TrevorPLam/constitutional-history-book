# CH06: Federalism as Shield: States' Rights Used to Block Federal Intervention

## Overview
The Supreme Court used federalism principles as a constitutional shield to block federal intervention in racial matters, expanding state authority to maintain white supremacist control while limiting federal civil rights protection.

## Primary Sources
### Federalism Shield
- **States' Rights Doctrine**: Court expanded state authority over racial matters under federalism.
- **Federal Power Limited**: Congressional authority to intervene in racial discrimination severely restricted.
- **State Autonomy Protected**: States received constitutional protection for racial segregation policies.

### Legal Framework
- **Dual Federalism**: Court reinforced strict separation between federal and state powers.
- **State Police Power**: States given broad authority to regulate race relations.
- **Federalism as Defense**: Federalism used to defend state racial discrimination from federal challenge.
