# CH06: Southern Resistance: Organized Opposition to Racial Equality

## Overview
Southern white resistance to racial equality was systematic, organized, and violent, creating political pressure that the Supreme Court would eventually accommodate through constitutional interpretation.

## Primary Sources
### Organized Resistance
- **Redeemer Movements**: Political campaigns to restore white Democratic control.
- **White Supremacist Organizations**: Ku Klux Klan and similar groups using terror.
- **Political Violence**: Systematic intimidation of Black voters and Republican officials.

### Resistance Strategies
- **Political Organization**: Southern whites organizing to regain political control.
- **Economic Coercion**: Economic pressure on Black citizens and white allies.
- **Legal Maneuvering**: Use of state laws to circumvent federal protections.
