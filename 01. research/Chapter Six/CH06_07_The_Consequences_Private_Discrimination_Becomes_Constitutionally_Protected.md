# CH06: The Consequences: Private Discrimination Becomes Constitutionally Protected

## Overview
The Civil Rights Cases and state action doctrine had devastating consequences, effectively making private racial discrimination constitutionally protected and removing federal authority to address the most common forms of racial oppression.

## Primary Sources
### Private Discrimination Protected
- **Public Accommodations**: Hotels, restaurants, theaters could discriminate without constitutional constraint.
- **Transportation**: Railroads and other carriers could segregate passengers legally.
- **Economic Segregation**: Private businesses could refuse service to Black customers constitutionally.

### Legal Consequences
- **Federal Powerlessness**: Federal government lost authority to combat private discrimination.
- **State Inaction**: States could allow private discrimination without constitutional violation.
- **Constitutional Shield**: Private discrimination received constitutional protection from federal regulation.
