# CH06: Lochner Era Beginnings: Substantive Due Process for Economic Liberty

## Overview
The Lochner era beginnings marked the Court's expansion of substantive due process to protect economic liberty, using the 14th Amendment to shield business interests from government regulation while simultaneously abandoning racial justice.

## Primary Sources
### Substantive Due Process
- **Economic Liberty Doctrine**: Court protected "freedom of contract" under 14th Amendment.
- **Business Regulation Struck Down**: Labor laws and regulations invalidated as unconstitutional.
- **Corporate Protection**: 14th Amendment applied to protect business rather than racial equality.

### Legal Framework
- **Liberty of Contract**: Court recognized constitutional right to contract without government interference.
- **Economic Due Process**: Due process clause applied to economic rather than racial protection.
- **Corporate Personhood**: Corporations received 14th Amendment protection as "persons."
