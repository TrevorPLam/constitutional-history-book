# CH06: The Doctrine: Separate but Equal as Constitutional Principle

## Overview
The "separate but equal" doctrine established in Plessy v. Ferguson became a constitutional principle that provided legal justification for racial segregation while maintaining the facade of constitutional equality.

## Primary Sources
### Separate but Equal Doctrine
- **Constitutional Principle**: Court elevated "separate but equal" to constitutional status.
- **Segregation Justification**: Decision provided constitutional rationale for racial separation.
- **Equality Facade**: Doctrine maintained appearance of equality while enabling substantive inequality.

### Legal Framework
- **Political vs. Social Equality**: Court distinguished between political equality (protected) and social equality (unprotected).
- **State Authority**: States given constitutional authority to enforce racial segregation.
- **Judicial Deference**: Court deferred to legislative judgment on racial matters.
