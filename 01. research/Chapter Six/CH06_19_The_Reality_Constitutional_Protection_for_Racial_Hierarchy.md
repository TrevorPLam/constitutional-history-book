# CH06: The Reality: Constitutional Protection for Racial Hierarchy

## Overview
The "separate but equal" doctrine in practice provided constitutional protection for racial hierarchy, enabling systematic segregation and discrimination while maintaining the facade of constitutional equality.

## Primary Sources
### Racial Hierarchy Protection
- **Constitutional Segregation**: Decision gave constitutional legitimacy to systematic racial separation.
- **Hierarchy Enforcement**: Court provided constitutional protection for white supremacist control.
- **Equality Illusion**: "Separate but equal" maintained appearance of equality while enabling substantive inequality.

### Practical Implementation
- **Jim Crow Laws**: Southern states enacted comprehensive segregation with constitutional protection.
- **Systematic Discrimination**: Racial hierarchy enforced across all aspects of public life.
- **Federal Protection Lost**: Federal government lost constitutional authority to challenge segregation.
