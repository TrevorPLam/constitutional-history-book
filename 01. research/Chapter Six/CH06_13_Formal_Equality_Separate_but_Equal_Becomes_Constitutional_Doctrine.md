# CH06: Formal Equality: Separate but Equal Becomes Constitutional Doctrine

## Overview
The Supreme Court developed the doctrine of "separate but equal" as a form of formal equality that constitutionalized racial segregation while abandoning the substantive equality promised by Reconstruction.

## Primary Sources
### Separate but Equal Doctrine
- **Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)**: Legalized racial segregation under "separate but equal" doctrine.
- **Formal Equality Principle**: Court upheld superficial equality while maintaining substantive inequality.
- **State Segregation Power**: States received constitutional authority to enforce racial segregation.

### Legal Framework
- **Equal Protection Inversion**: Equal protection clause applied to justify rather than prevent racial discrimination.
- **Separate Facilities**: Constitutionality of segregated public facilities established.
- **State Action Expansion**: State power to segregate expanded under constitutional protection.
