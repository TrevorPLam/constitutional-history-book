# CH06: Corporate Personhood: 14th Amendment Protects Business Interests

## Overview
The Supreme Court extended 14th Amendment protection to corporations as "persons," using the amendment designed to protect freed slaves to shield business interests from government regulation.

## Primary Sources
### Corporate Personhood Doctrine
- **Santa Clara County v. Southern Pacific (1886)**: Court recognized corporations as "persons" under 14th Amendment.
- **Equal Protection Applied**: Corporations received equal protection clause benefits.
- **Business Interests Shielded**: Corporate property and contract rights protected constitutionally.

### Legal Framework
- **Corporate Constitutional Rights**: Corporations gained constitutional protections originally intended for freed slaves.
- **Property Protection**: Corporate property received 14th Amendment protection.
- **Contract Freedom**: Corporate contracts shielded from government interference.
