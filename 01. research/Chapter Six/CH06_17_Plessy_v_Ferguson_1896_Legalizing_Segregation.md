# CH06: Plessy v. Ferguson (1896): Legalizing Segregation

## Overview
Plessy v. Ferguson (1896) represented the culmination of the Supreme Court's betrayal of Reconstruction, constitutionalizing racial segregation through the "separate but equal" doctrine and completing the transformation of the 14th Amendment from shield to sword.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Plessy v. Ferguson**: 163 U.S. 537 (1896) - upheld Louisiana segregation statute.
- **Separate but Equal Doctrine**: Court established constitutional principle of racial segregation.
- **Racial Hierarchy Legalized**: Decision gave constitutional legitimacy to Jim Crow segregation.

### Legal Reasoning
- **Social Equality Distinction**: Court distinguished between political and social equality.
- **State Police Power**: States given broad authority to regulate racial relations.
- **Precedent Established**: Decision created foundation for half-century of segregation.
