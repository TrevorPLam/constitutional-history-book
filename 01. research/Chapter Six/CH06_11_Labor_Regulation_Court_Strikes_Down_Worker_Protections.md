# CH06: Labor Regulation: Court Strikes Down Worker Protections

## Overview
The Supreme Court systematically struck down labor regulations and worker protections using substantive due process, prioritizing business interests over workers' rights and safety.

## Primary Sources
### Labor Regulation Struck Down
- **Working Hour Limits**: Court invalidated laws limiting working hours as unconstitutional.
- **Minimum Wage Laws**: Wage regulation struck down as interference with contract freedom.
- **Worker Safety Regulations**: Occupational safety laws invalidated under substantive due process.

### Legal Framework
- **Freedom of Contract**: Court protected employers' and employees' "freedom" to contract without government regulation.
- **Economic Due Process**: Due process clause applied to protect business from labor regulation.
- **Business Interests Prioritized**: Corporate economic interests given constitutional priority over worker protection.
