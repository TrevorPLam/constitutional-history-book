# CH04: Constitutional Issues: Citizenship, Territorial Power, Missouri Compromise

## Overview
The Dred Scott case raised fundamental constitutional questions about citizenship, federal territorial power, and the constitutionality of the Missouri Compromise. These issues touched the core of American federalism and sectional conflict.

## Primary Sources
### Citizenship Question
- **Article III**: Standing requirements for federal court jurisdiction.
- **Diversity Jurisdiction**: Constitutional basis for Scott's federal lawsuit.
- **Citizenship Definition**: Constitutional meaning of "citizen" for African Americans.

### Territorial Power
- **Article IV**: Congressional power over territories.
- **Missouri Compromise**: 1820 law restricting slavery in territories.
- **Property Rights**: Fifth Amendment protection of slave property.

### Constitutional Conflicts
- **Federal vs. State**: Tension between federal and state power over slavery.
- **Territorial Authority**: Scope of congressional power in territories.
- **Individual Rights**: Constitutional protections for enslaved persons.
