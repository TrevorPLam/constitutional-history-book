# CH04: Legal Background: Scott's Claim to Freedom Through Residence in Free Territory

## Overview
Dred Scott's legal claim to freedom was based on residence in free territory, following established legal precedents. The case presented complex constitutional questions about citizenship, territorial power, and federal authority over slavery.

## Primary Sources
### Scott's Legal Journey
- **Residence in Free Territory**: Scott lived in Illinois (free state) and Wisconsin Territory (free by Missouri Compromise).
- **Legal Precedents**: Previous cases supporting freedom claims based on free residence.
- **Missouri Compromise**: 1820 law prohibiting slavery in certain territories.

### Legal Arguments
- **Freedom Suits**: Scott filed multiple lawsuits claiming freedom based on residence.
- **State Court Victory**: Initially won freedom in Missouri court (1850).
- **Federal Appeal**: Case appealed to federal courts, eventually reaching Supreme Court.
