# CH04: Political Stakes: Slavery Expansion, Sectional Balance, National Unity

## Overview
The Dred Scott case carried enormous political stakes involving slavery expansion, sectional balance in Congress, and the future of national unity. The Court's decision threatened to reshape the entire political landscape of the United States.

## Primary Sources
### Slavery Expansion
- **Territorial Question**: Whether slavery could expand into new territories.
- **Popular Sovereignty**: Stephen Douglas's doctrine of local decision-making.
- **Free Soil Movement**: Northern opposition to slavery expansion.

### Sectional Balance
- **Senate Representation**: Equal balance between slave and free states.
- **Missouri Compromise**: Maintained sectional equilibrium since 1820.
- **Kansas-Nebraska Act**: Disrupted balance, led to sectional conflict.

### National Unity
- **Union Preservation**: Threat of disunion over slavery question.
- **Political Compromise**: Failure of previous compromise attempts.
- **Sectional Tensions**: Growing North-South polarization.

## Historian Analyses

- **James McPherson**: In "Battle Cry of Freedom" (1988), McPherson emphasizes the "existential stakes" of the slavery question.
- **David Potter**: In "The Impending Crisis" (1976), Potter analyzes the "sectional balance" as central to national politics.
- **Eric Foner**: In "Free Soil, Free Labor, Free Men" (1970), Foner examines the "political dimensions" of slavery expansion.

### Sectional Conflict
- **Sean Wilentz**: In "The Rise of American Democracy" (2005), Wilentz emphasizes the "sectional breakdown" of national politics.
- **Daniel Walker Howe**: In "What Hath God Wrought" (2007), Howe analyzes the "political crisis" of the 1850s.
- **Michael F. Holt**: In "The Political Crisis of the 1850s" (1978), Holt discusses the "collapse of the second party system."

### National Unity
- **William Freehling**: In "The Road to Disunion" (1990), Freehling examines the "disunion threat" over slavery.
- **Kenneth M. Stampp**: In "The Causes of the Civil War" (1965), Stampp analyzes the "national unity" question.
- **Avery Craven**: In "The Coming of the Civil War" (1950), Craven discusses the "union preservation" issue.

### Political Consequences
- **Don E. Fehrenbacher**: In "The Dred Scott Case" (1978), Fehrenbacher analyzes the "political impact" of the decision.
- **Vincent C. Hopkins**: In "Dred Scott's Case" (1967), Hopkins examines the "political consequences" of the ruling.
- **Robert Remini**: In "Henry Clay" (1991), Remini discusses the "political compromise" failures.
