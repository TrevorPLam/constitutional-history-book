# CH04: War as Resolution: Political Violence Settles What Judicial Decision Could Not

## Overview
The Civil War ultimately resolved the slavery question that the Dred Scott decision attempted to settle through judicial decree. Political violence accomplished what judicial supremacy could not - ending slavery and preserving the Union.

## Primary Sources
### War Resolution
- **Civil War (1861-1865)**: Military conflict resolved sectional issues.
- **Emancipation Proclamation (1863)**: Executive action ended slavery in rebel states.
- **Union Victory**: Military defeat of Confederacy settled constitutional questions.

### Judicial Failure
- **Decision Ineffectiveness**: Dred Scott ruling proved powerless to resolve conflict.
- **Political Resolution**: War, not courts, settled fundamental questions.
- **Constitutional Change**: Military victory enabled constitutional revolution.
