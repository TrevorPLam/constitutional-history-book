# CH04: The Decision: Full Opinion and Immediate Political Impact

## Overview
Chief Justice Roger Taney's Dred Scott opinion, issued March 6, 1857, represented the most controversial Supreme Court decision in American history. The ruling immediately transformed the political landscape and intensified sectional conflict.

## Primary Sources
### Taney's Opinion
- **Majority Decision**: 7-2 ruling denying Scott's freedom claim.
- **Citizenship Holding**: African Americans could not be U.S. citizens.
- **Missouri Compromise**: Declared unconstitutional as deprivation of property.
- **Broad Scope**: Went beyond case facts to address broader slavery questions.

### Immediate Impact
- **Northern Outrage**: Widespread condemnation and protest.
- **Southern Celebration**: Enthusiastic endorsement of pro-slavery victory.
- **Political Polarization**: Exacerbated sectional tensions and conflict.
