# CH04: The Lesson: Judicial Supremacy Requires Institutional Restraint

## Overview
The ultimate lesson of the Dred Scott decision is that judicial supremacy requires institutional restraint. The Court's attempt to resolve political questions through judicial overreach destroyed its authority and demonstrated the necessity of constitutional humility.

## Primary Sources
### Institutional Restraint
- **Marshall's Wisdom**: Strategic avoidance of political questions preserved Court authority.
- **Taney's Error**: Judicial overreach destroyed institutional legitimacy.
- **Constitutional Humility**: Recognition of judicial limitations essential to authority.

### Historical Lesson
- **Power Limits**: Judicial power depends on political acceptance and institutional restraint.
- **Neutrality Principle**: Court must remain neutral arbiter rather than political combatant.
- **Constitutional Role**: Judicial authority requires respect for democratic processes.
