# CH04: The Irony: Judicial Supremacy Destroys Judicial Authority

## Overview
The supreme irony of the Dred Scott decision was that the Court's assertion of judicial supremacy ultimately destroyed its institutional authority. By overreaching its constitutional role, the Court undermined the very foundation of its power.

## Primary Sources
### Supreme Irony
- **Authority Claim**: Court asserted final constitutional authority.
- **Authority Loss**: Decision destroyed Court's institutional legitimacy.
- **Supremacy Failure**: Judicial supremacy proved self-defeating.

### Institutional Destruction
- **Legitimacy Foundation**: Authority based on public acceptance and neutrality.
- **Legitimacy Loss**: Political action destroyed institutional credibility.
- **Authority Vacuum**: Court created institutional crisis through overreach.
