# CH05: United States v. Reese (1876): Limiting 15th Amendment Enforcement

## Overview
United States v. Reese (1876) significantly limited 15th Amendment enforcement by allowing states to use ostensibly neutral voting qualifications to disenfranchise Black voters, undermining federal protection of voting rights.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **United States v. Reese**: 92 U.S. 214 (1876) - narrowed 15th Amendment enforcement.
- **Neutral Qualifications**: Court upheld state voting qualifications that had discriminatory effects.
- **Intent Requirement**: Decision required proof of discriminatory intent, not just discriminatory impact.

### Legal Consequences
- **Voting Rights Undermined**: Decision allowed circumvention of 15th Amendment guarantees.
- **State Authority Expanded**: Court granted states broad authority to regulate voting.
- **Federal Protection Limited**: Federal government's power to protect voting rights severely restricted.
