# CH05: The Pattern: Court Systematically Dismantles Reconstruction Protections

## Overview
The Supreme Court established a clear pattern of systematically dismantling Reconstruction protections through a series of decisions that limited federal authority and expanded state power over civil rights and voting rights.

## Primary Sources
### Systematic Dismantling
- **Slaughterhouse Cases (1873)**: Narrowed 14th Amendment privileges or immunities clause.
- **United States v. Cruikshank (1876)**: Limited federal civil rights enforcement.
- **United States v. Reese (1876)**: Restricted 15th Amendment voting rights protection.

### Judicial Pattern
- **Consistent Direction**: Court consistently ruled against federal civil rights protection.
- **State Authority Expansion**: Decisions systematically expanded state power over individual rights.
- **Federal Power Contraction**: Court progressively limited federal authority to protect civil rights.
