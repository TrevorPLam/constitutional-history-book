# CH05: Judicial Role: Court as Enforcer of New Constitutional Order

## Overview
The Supreme Court faced a crucial role in enforcing the new constitutional order created by the Reconstruction Amendments. The Court's interpretation and enforcement would determine whether the revolutionary promises of the amendments would become reality or remain aspirational.

## Primary Sources
### Constitutional Enforcement
- **Judicial Review**: Court's power to interpret and enforce Reconstruction amendments.
- **Federal Authority**: 14th Amendment empowered federal courts to protect individual rights.
- **State Action Doctrine**: Court's role in regulating state compliance with new requirements.

### Institutional Responsibility
- **Constitutional Guardian**: Court as protector of newly established rights.
- **Federal Enforcement**: Judicial power to ensure state compliance with amendments.
- **Rights Protection**: Court's duty to enforce equal protection and due process guarantees.
