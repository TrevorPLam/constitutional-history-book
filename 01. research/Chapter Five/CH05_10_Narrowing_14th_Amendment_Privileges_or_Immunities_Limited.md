# CH05: Narrowing 14th Amendment: Privileges or Immunities Limited

## Overview
The Supreme Court's narrow interpretation of the 14th Amendment's privileges or immunities clause in the Slaughterhouse Cases severely limited federal protection of civil rights and undermined the amendment's revolutionary purpose.

## Primary Sources
### Privileges or Immunities Clause
- **Narrow Interpretation**: Court limited clause to rights of national citizenship only.
- **State Citizenship Rights**: Most rights protected under state citizenship remained with states.
- **Federal Protection Limited**: Federal authority to protect individual rights severely restricted.

### Legal Consequences
- **Civil Rights Protection**: Decision undermined federal civil rights enforcement.
- **State Authority**: Preserved broad state police powers over individual rights.
- **Amendment Purpose**: Formalistic interpretation defeated amendment's democratic purpose.
