# CH05: Democratic Promise: Judicial Power as Shield for Newly Freed Citizens

## Overview
The early Reconstruction Court demonstrated how judicial power could serve as a shield for newly freed citizens, protecting their constitutional rights and ensuring their participation in American democracy.

## Primary Sources
### Rights Protection
- **Citizenship Protection**: Court enforced 14th Amendment citizenship guarantees.
- **Equal Protection**: Judicial shield against discriminatory state laws.
- **Voting Rights**: Court protection of 15th Amendment voting guarantees.

### Democratic Shield
- **Federal Protection**: Judicial enforcement of federal civil rights guarantees.
- **State Action Limitation**: Court limited state power to infringe on individual rights.
- **Constitutional Guardian**: Court as protector of vulnerable minority rights.
