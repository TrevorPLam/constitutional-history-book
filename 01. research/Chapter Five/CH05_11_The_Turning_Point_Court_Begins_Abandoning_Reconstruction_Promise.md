# CH05: The Turning Point: Court Begins Abandoning Reconstruction Promise

## Overview
The Slaughterhouse Cases marked a crucial turning point as the Supreme Court began abandoning the Reconstruction promise of racial equality and federal protection of civil rights.

## Primary Sources
### Judicial Retreat
- **Doctrinal Shift**: Court moved from protecting to limiting civil rights.
- **Federal Authority**: Decision restricted federal power to protect individual rights.
- **State Power**: Decision preserved broad state authority over civil rights.

### Constitutional Reversal
- **Amendment Purpose**: Formalistic interpretation defeated Reconstruction's democratic purpose.
- **Rights Protection**: Court retreated from active protection of civil rights.
- **Political Context**: Decision reflected growing Northern retreat from Reconstruction.
