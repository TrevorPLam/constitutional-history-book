# CH05: Initial Enthusiasm: Judicial Enforcement of Reconstruction Amendments

## Overview
The early Reconstruction Court showed initial enthusiasm for enforcing the Reconstruction amendments, embracing its role as protector of newly established civil rights and voting rights for formerly enslaved persons.

## Primary Sources
### Early Enforcement
- **Initial Decisions**: Court upheld Reconstruction-era civil rights legislation.
- **Rights Protection**: Early cases enforced equal protection and due process guarantees.
- **Voting Rights**: Court initially protected 15th Amendment voting rights provisions.

### Judicial Commitment
- **Constitutional Revolution**: Court embraced transformative purpose of amendments.
- **Rights Enforcement**: Active judicial protection of newly established civil rights.
- **Democratic Protection**: Court as guardian of Reconstruction's democratic promises.
