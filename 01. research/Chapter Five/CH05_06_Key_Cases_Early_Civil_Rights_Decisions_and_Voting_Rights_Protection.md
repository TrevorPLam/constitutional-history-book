# CH05: Key Cases: Early Civil Rights Decisions and Voting Rights Protection

## Overview
The early Reconstruction Court issued key decisions protecting civil rights and voting rights, demonstrating judicial commitment to enforcing the revolutionary promises of the Reconstruction amendments.

## Primary Sources
### Civil Rights Cases
- **Strauder v. West Virginia (1880)**: Court struck down racial exclusion from juries.
- **Ex parte Virginia (1879)**: Court upheld federal authority to protect civil rights.
- **United States v. Hall (1871)**: Early recognition of equal protection principles.

### Voting Rights Protection
- **United States v. Reese (1876)**: Initially protected voting rights before later retreat.
- **Early Enforcement**: Court upheld congressional power to protect voting rights.
- **15th Amendment Cases**: Early decisions enforcing voting rights guarantees.
