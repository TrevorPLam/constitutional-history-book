# CH05: The Slaughterhouse Cases (1873): Beginning of Judicial Retreat

## Overview
The Slaughterhouse Cases (1873) marked the beginning of the Supreme Court's retreat from Reconstruction, narrowly interpreting the 14th Amendment's privileges or immunities clause and limiting federal protection of civil rights.

## Primary Sources
### The Decision
- **Slaughterhouse Cases**: 83 U.S. 36 (1873) - narrow interpretation of 14th Amendment.
- **Privileges or Immunities**: Court limited clause to rights of national citizenship.
- **State Authority**: Decision preserved broad state police powers.

### Legal Reasoning
- **Narrow Construction**: Court restricted 14th Amendment scope.
- **Federalism Emphasis**: Decision prioritized state authority over federal protection.
- **Formalistic Interpretation**: Technical reading undermined amendment's purpose.
