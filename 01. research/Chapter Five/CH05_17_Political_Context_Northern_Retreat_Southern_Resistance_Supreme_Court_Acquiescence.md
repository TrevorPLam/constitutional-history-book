# CH05: Political Context: Northern Retreat, Southern Resistance, Supreme Court Acquiescence

## Overview
The Supreme Court's retreat from Reconstruction occurred within a broader political context of Northern retreat from Reconstruction, Southern resistance to civil rights, and judicial acquiescence to white supremacist restoration.

## Primary Sources
### Northern Retreat
- **Political Will Declining**: Northern public and political support for Reconstruction diminished.
- **Economic Priorities**: Northern focus shifted from civil rights to economic development.
- **War Weariness**: Growing Northern fatigue with continued Southern intervention.

### Southern Resistance
- **White Supremacist Opposition**: Organized Southern resistance to Reconstruction and civil rights.
- **Violence and Intimidation**: Systematic use of violence to undermine Black political participation.
- **Political Organization**: Southern "Redeemer" movements to restore white control.

### Judicial Acquiescence
- **Court Alignment**: Supreme Court decisions aligned with Northern retreat and Southern resistance.
- **Constitutional Justification**: Court provided constitutional rationale for abandoning Reconstruction.
- **Institutional Complicity**: Judicial branch facilitated white supremacist restoration.
